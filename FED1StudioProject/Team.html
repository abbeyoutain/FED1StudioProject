Leadership

Emma Woodhouse

At twenty-one, Emma Woodhouse is the youngest president the academy has had. Handsome and clever with a happy disposition, Emma has very little to distress or vex her, but much still does. Emma lives with her father in their home Hartfield in the English countryside near the village of Highbury. In her free time, she practices playing the role of matchmaker amongst her friends and acquaintances, much to their chagrin.

Elinor Dashwood

Elinor Dashwood, a reserved, practical, and thoughtful young woman, possesses a coolness of judgement and strength of understanding. Elinor’s way of understanding the world is based upon careful observation of the character of others. She is also a frequent counselor to others, showing common sense and thoughtfulness when others experience the extreme throws of emotions.

Elizabeth Bennet

Elizabeth Bennet, known as Lizzie by her friends and family, is the second of five sisters. She lives in the Longbourn estate, situated near the market village of Meryton in Hertfordshire. While quite young at twenty years old, she is an intelligent young woman, with a lively, playful disposition, and a good-natured impertinence without being offensive.

Sales

Dorothy Gale

Raised by her aunt and uncle in Kansas alongside her dog Toto, Dorothy has a forthright and take-charge character. In life, she exhibits little fear, slapping lions, defeating winged monkeys, and hurling water at wicked witches.

Marianne Dashwood

Marianne Dashwood embraces spontaneity, excessive sensibility, love of nature, and romantic idealism. To the people she cares for, she loves with a warmth that leaps over all barriers, even those of propriety. Her sorrows, joys, and love have no moderation. She lives life to the fullest.

Susan Pevensie

Susan, the second-oldest of four siblings, is a maternal, practical, and cautious young woman. Though she may not be the most adventurous, she is kind. She also has a real talent for archery.

Administration

Jane Bennet

Jane Bennet is largely considered to be the most beautiful young lady in any neighborhood, but more importantly, she is the most kind young lady in any neighborhood. Her steady, genteel disposition, and her sweet, shy sensibilities make her an excellent friend, and she always sees the good in others.

Charlotte Lucas

A sensible, intelligent woman, Charlotte Lucas is quite pragmatic, sometimes to the point of cynicism. She does not care to consider herself a romantic, but she can be counted on to make the best of things.

Lucy Pevensie

The youngest of four siblings, Lucy Pevensie is known to be naive, but more importantly, she is in touch with wonder and her ability to believe in goodness. Lucy is deeply kind, inquisitive, open, and she is a good, loyal friend.

Instructors

Mary Poppins

A quintessential English nanny, Mary Poppins is a slightly stern but caring woman, who uses magic and self-control to care for children. She is kind towards children, but can be firm when needed. She is recognizable by her sensible hat and parrot umbrella.

Jane Eyre

Though plain, Jane Eyre is a passionate and strongly principled person, and values freedom and independence. Jane repeatedly succeeds at asserting herself and maintains her principles of justice, human dignity, and morality. She also values intellectual and emotional fulfillment.

Anne Shirley

Although Anne Shirley can be known to be stubborn and passionate, she is fiercely loyal and intelligent. She loves beauty and fantasy, but she also appreciates the simplicity of her life and prefers it to riches. Although her imagination still serves her well, she loves the world as it is.
